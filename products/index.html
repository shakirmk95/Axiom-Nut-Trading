Dry Fruits
Almonds

Almonds or PrunusDulcis is a highly nutritious nut.They are a rich source of vitamin E, calcium, phosphorous, iron and magnesium. Usually connected to higher intellectual level, they help in the development and health of human brain. Apart from this, they also help in reducing cholesterol,nourish skin and boost energy as well as immune system.

Cashews

The delicately flavoured cashew nut is a favourite between meal and snack. The health benefits of cashews include a healthy heart, strong muscle function, an improved bone and oral health among others. They also provide a relief from diabetes, anaemia, and gallstones.

Brazil Nuts

They are the number one food source for selenium, an extremely vital mineral for human immunity.They also help in preventing cancer , as well lifting moods, hence they are an excellent addition to depression diet.

Hazel Nuts

Who doesn’t love hazelnut flavour in coffee or chocolates! It’s a versatile nut which can be enjoyed raw, roasted, or as an ingredient in variety of dishes. It also helps in managing diabetes , controlling cholesterol and boosting the functioning of brain.

Macadamia Nuts

Native to Australia, these are considered as the world’s finest nuts. Their buttery flavour not only takes care of your taste buds but also your body. Packed with fiber, iron and healthy fats, they're the perfect treat for snacking, baking or cooking.

Walnuts

King of dry fruits, walnut can provide numerous health benefits at very mild doses.They have cancer fighting properties, aid in weight loss and supports healthful cholesterol levels. Snack on them out of the bag, or toss them in salads and baked goods for more protein, fiber and omega-3s.

This vibrant fruitis packed with fiber, antioxidants and potassium for a healthy snack. Known for their ability to treat indigestion, earaches, fever, skin diseases, cancers, and anemia, apricots are small dupes which closly relate to peaches. Apart from direct consumption they are also used in jams, squash, juices and jellies.

Dates

Dates are always known for their healing powers.The health benefits of dates are many, and popping one every day will only add to your health. This edible sweet fruit, is grown abundantly in Middle Eastern countries, and is best served with lots of love!Naturally flavorful and low-in-fat, dates are a healthy way to satisfy a sweet craving.

Figs

A fig is a soft and sweet bulbous fruit that comes from various species of Ficus trees. Figs contain hundreds of tiny edible seeds that are appreciated for the texture and nutrients they add to the fruit. They are considered exotic and consumed all over the world in both fresh and dried form, and can be enjoyed as a healthy snack or added to savory and sweet recipes.

Prunes

Delicious prunes which are ideal for cooking and snacking. Not only do prunes taste great, but they're also very healthy - naturally sweet, fat free and high in potassium and fiber (4 grams of fiber per serving)! Prunes have acted as a popular digestive remedy for decades.

Cherries

This bright fruit is not only appealing to the eyes, but also to our taste buds. Cherries are a sweet, tangy, and flavorful treat that is great for snacking, cooking, and baking. Cherries are rich in antioxidants and heart-healthy nutrients.Bursting with vitamins and antioxidants, they are known for reducing inflammation as well as blood pressure of regularly eaten.
